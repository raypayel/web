Famous Flowers of India

The Lotus (Kamal)

The Lotus, known as "Kamal" in Hindi, is the national flower of India. It symbolizes purity, beauty, wealth, and spirituality. Growing in muddy water, it emerges to bloom beautifully, representing enlightenment and divine beauty.

Marigold (Genda)

The Marigold, or "Genda", is a vibrant flower widely used in Indian festivals and religious ceremonies. It symbolizes auspiciousness and is often associated with the celebration of life and prosperity.

Jasmine (Chameli)

Jasmine, or "Chameli", is a sweet-smelling flower that holds a significant place in Indian culture and traditions. Known for its delicate fragrance, it is often used in garlands and perfumes, symbolizing grace and elegance.

Hibiscus (Gudhal)

The Hibiscus, or "Gudhal", is commonly found in Indian gardens and is used in various traditional remedies. It represents beauty and is also associated with the goddess Kali, symbolizing strength and transformation.

Sunflower (Surajmukhi)

The Sunflower, or "Surajmukhi", is known for its bright yellow petals and its tendency to turn towards the sun. It symbolizes adoration and loyalty, and is often seen in gardens and fields across India.
